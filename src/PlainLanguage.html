Plain Language Guidelines

  • Remove passive voice.
  • Find hidden verbs.
  • Simplify verbs.
  • Use contractions.
  • Add "must" when required.
  • De-noun the verbs.
  • Add pronouns.
  • Minimize abbreviations.
  • Simplify words used.
  • Remove unnecessary words.
  • Remove jargon, technical terms, etc.

Sentences

  • Some companies know sustainability is important but still focus on quick profits.
  • The committee decided to delay the community center renovation.
  • If the weather is good, the solar panels will work by the end of the quarter.
  • The development team fixed early problems for project success.
  • Event organizers overcame challenges to host a successful festival.

Verbs Demystified

Active vs. Passive Voice

  • Active: Subject performs action (e.g., "The cat chased the mouse").
  • Passive: Action performed by subject (e.g., "The mouse was chased by the cat").

Verb Forms

  • Simple: Present (walk), Past (walked), Future (will walk)
  • Continuous: Present (is walking), Past (was walking), Future (will be walking)
  • Perfect: Present (has walked), Past (had walked), Future (will have walked)
  • Perfect Continuous: Present (has been walking), Past (had been walking), Future (will have been walking)

Examples

Words

Original Sentence: The report on the environmental impact has been completed by the researchers.

Revised Sentence: Researchers completed the environmental impact report.

Reasoning:

  • Removed passive voice.
  • Simplified verbs.
  • Removed unnecessary words.
  • Added pronouns.

Sentences

Original Sentence: Despite the widespread recognition of the importance of sustainability in corporate practices, there are numerous organizations that continue to prioritize short-term profits, which ultimately detracts from the long-term viability of both the companies and the communities in which they operate.

Revised Sentence: Many companies know sustainability is important but still focus on quick profits, which harms long-term viability.

Reasoning:

  • Shortened the sentence.
  • Main idea first.
  • Removed unnecessary words.

Paragraphs

Original Paragraph: The environmental impacts of urbanization have been significant, particularly concerning the increase in air pollution levels and the reduction in green spaces due to the expansion of infrastructure. Cities have been experiencing a rise in air pollutants resulting from increased vehicular emissions and industrial activities. This has been exacerbated by the reduction in natural environments, which has decreased the capacity for city landscapes to filter out pollution naturally. Noise pollution has also become predominant, with the increasing population density leading to a surge in transportation noise and general urban activity. These environmental challenges have prompted cities to adopt various measures aimed at mitigating their impacts, such as the implementation of green roofs and the creation of urban parks. Moreover, public transportation systems have been expanded and improved to reduce reliance on personal vehicles. Efforts to promote sustainable practices are continuing, as are initiatives to enhance urban resilience in the face of environmental challenges.

Revised Paragraph: Urbanization increases air pollution and reduces green spaces. Cities face higher pollutants from more vehicles and industries, worsened by fewer natural areas to filter pollution. Noise pollution has risen with population density. Cities mitigate impacts with green roofs, parks, and expanded public transport. Sustainable practices enhance urban resilience.

Reasoning:

  • Created a topic sentence.
  • Used transition words.
  • Shortened the paragraph.
  • Kept one topic per paragraph.

General Clarity Aids

Original Sentence: The concert will take place on Saturday, weather permitting, and is anticipated to be attended by approximately 200 individuals.

Revised Sentence: Saturday's concert is expected to have about 200 people attend.

Reasoning:

  • Removed passive voice.
  • Removed unnecessary words.
  • Simplified words used.
  • Simplified verbs.
  • Shortened the sentence.
