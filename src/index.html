Welcome to My Accessibility Website

In 2021, a survey found that 13% of Americans have a disability. About 38.9 million adults in the U.S. have a disability, and 19.7% of them are poor. This is almost twice the number of non-disabled adults who are poor. One reason for this is that fewer disabled people have jobs. In the U.S., 25.9% of disabled people over age 16 have jobs, compared to 59.6% of all Americans over age 16.

In Utah, 10.3% of people have a disability, which is about 342,378 people. In the whole U.S., 13% of people have a disability, which is about 42.5 million people. There are differences between genders and types of disabilities in Utah and the U.S. There are several reasons for these differences, but that's not the focus here. When looking at disabilities by race, Utah and the U.S. have some differences. Universal Design is Design for All

Tim Berners-Lee, who created the web, knew that making the web accessible to everyone is important: "The power of the web is in its universality. Access by everyone regardless is an essential aspect."

This website follows the principles of Universal Design by Ronald Mace and his team:

  • Equitable Use: The design is useful for people with different abilities.
  • Flexibility in Use: The design suits different preferences and abilities.
  • Simple and Intuitive Use: The design is easy to understand, no matter the user's experience or skills.
  • Perceptible Information: The design shares information effectively, regardless of the user's senses or environment.
  • Tolerance for Error: The design reduces risks and consequences of mistakes.
  • Low Physical Effort: The design is comfortable to use with little effort.
  • Size and Space for Approach and Use: The design allows easy access and use, regardless of body size or mobility.

This website also meets the Level AA requirements of WCAG 2.1 for web accessibility.
